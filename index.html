Mental Health

    In our fast-paced world, taking a momentary pause is essential to prioritize mental well-being.
    Mental health isn't just the absence of illness; it involves cultivating resilience, managing stress, and establishing a fulfilling life foundation.
    Beyond managing conditions, mental health is a key factor in building a foundation for a satisfying and balanced life.

  • Overview
  • About

Mental health is a state of well-being that affects how individuals think, feel, and act. It encompasses emotional, psychological, and social aspects of life. Factors such as genetics, biology, environment, and life experiences contribute to mental health.

It's essential to prioritize mental health through practices like self-care, stress management, and seeking support when needed. Promoting awareness and reducing stigma surrounding mental health are crucial for fostering a supportive community.

Mental health conditions are common and can affect anyone. They range from mood disorders like depression and anxiety to more severe conditions such as schizophrenia. Seeking professional help, such as therapy and counseling, is essential for managing mental health challenges.

Education, open conversations, and creating a supportive environment contribute to breaking down stigma associated with mental health. Understanding that mental health is a continuum and acknowledging the importance of well-being are crucial steps toward a healthier society.

How To Overcome Anxiety

Anxiety and stress are no strangers to individuals living in the digital age, with the constant need for social validation, comparing oneself to others triggered by what one sees on social media, performance stress, work pressures.

What is Anxiety?

Everyone goes through some amount of anxiety throughout their lives. It could be because of a final exam, a new job, a move to a new city or your child’s whereabouts. This is completely normal, and occasionally anxiety can also prove to be useful.

Strength in Solidarity

In a world that often emphasizes individuality, it is crucial to recognize the power of solidarity and support, especially regarding mental health. Mental health issues can affect anyone, including people in our closest circles.

Depression

Postpartum Depression

Postpartum depression is a mental health disorder that can affect mothers within the first twelve weeks of childbirth In India, It has been estimated that nearly 20% of mothers suffer from postpartum depression.

Depression in the Elderly

Mental health and well-being, an important factor during childhood and adulthood, is often overlooked as a health concern during old age. On the contrary, ageing leads to a higher risk of developing psychological problems.

Depression and Anxiety

Men tend to be diagnosed with depression less often than women. While this could partly be attributed to genetics and biology, there are other reasons as to why men are not diagnosed with depression as often as women.

Guided Mindfulness Exercise

Welcome to this mindfulness exercise. Find a comfortable and quiet place to sit or lie down.

Step 1: Close your eyes and take a few deep breaths. Inhale deeply through your nose, hold for a moment, and exhale slowly through your mouth.

Step 2: Bring your attention to the sensations of your breath. Notice the rise and fall of your chest or the sensation of the breath as it enters and leaves your nostrils.

Step 3: If your mind starts to wander, gently bring it back to the present moment by focusing on your breath.

Step 4: Expand your awareness to your body. Notice any areas of tension or discomfort, and allow them to relax as you continue to breathe deeply.

Step 5: Now, expand your awareness to the sounds around you. Notice any sounds without judgment, letting them come and go.

Step 6: Take a moment to express gratitude for this present moment and the opportunity to practice mindfulness.

Step 7: Focus on your breath, allowing each inhale and exhale to bring a sense of calmness.

Step 8: Scan your body for any areas of tension. Release and relax those areas as you continue to breathe.

Step 9: Bring your attention to the present moment by noticing the sensations of your surroundings.

Step 10: Gradually open your eyes and transition back to your daily activities with a sense of mindfulness.

Mental health is essential to a person’s life in the same way as physical health. Hesitation to talk about mental health adds to the notion that the topic is taboo. It is important to normalize conversations surrounding mental health so people can feel empowered to seek the help they need. Myths and facts about mental health
Myth: Mental health issues can't affect me.
Fact: Mental health issues can affect anyone. In 2020, about:
  • One in 6 young people have experienced a major depressive episode
  • Mental health issues can affect anyone, regardless of age, gender, socio-economic status, or background.
  • Children, adolescents, and adults can all experience mental health challenges. Recognizing and addressing these issues early in life can have significant positive effects.
Myth: I can't do anything for a person with a mental health issue.
Fact: Friends and loved ones can make a big difference.
  • Reaching out and letting them know you are available to help
  • Helping them access mental health services
  • Treating them with respect, just as you would anyone else
What are the common signs of mental health issues?
Changes in mood, energy levels, sleep patterns, appetite, and social withdrawal are common signs. Persistent feelings of sadness, anxiety, or difficulty coping are also indicators.

How can I improve my mental health?
Practices such as regular exercise, a balanced diet, sufficient sleep, social connections, and stress management can contribute to better mental health.
Read More
Can mental health issues be prevented?
While not all mental health issues can be prevented, adopting a healthy lifestyle, managing stress, and seeking support when needed can contribute to prevention.
Read More
Is it normal to seek help for mental health concerns?
Absolutely. Seeking help for mental health is a sign of strength. Professional support, such as counseling or therapy, can provide valuable tools for managing challenges.
Read More
How do I talk to someone about their mental health?
Approach the conversation with empathy, listen actively, express concern without judgment, and offer support. Encourage them to seek professional help if needed. Some mental health conditions may have a genetic component, but environmental factors also play a significant role.
